Why I Ride

There's a specific kind of silence you only get at the top of a run before you drop in — a few seconds where your whole brain narrows to terrain, speed, and the line you've already committed to. I've been chasing that feeling since I was a kid riding Colorado bumps with my family. Snowboarding taught me something I still use every day: once you've read the slope and decided, hesitation is the most dangerous thing you can do. Commit, adjust on the way down, and stick the landing. The mountains have a way of making that lesson very literal, very fast. Whether I'm at Brighton in the Wasatch or dodging trees at Steamboat, being out there resets everything — and reminds me that some of the best decisions are the ones made at speed.

My Pre-Run Checklist

Every good powder day starts the night before. Here's what I actually do:

  1. Check the snow report (Onthesnow, resort app, or OpenSnow)
  2. Pack the bag — don't forget:
    • Goggles + spare lens (always bring a low-light backup)
    • Hand warmers (at least two pairs)
    • Snacks — jerky, dark chocolate, a granola bar
    • Buff / balaclava if it's under 15°F
    • Sunscreen — altitude UV is no joke
  3. Wax the board or check the base (stone grind before season, hot wax every 3–4 days)
  4. Set the binding angles and check strap tightness
  5. Layers: base layer + mid layer + shell. No cotton.
  6. Leave the house early — 7:30 AM or the parking situation gets ugly
